How would you like to pay?

In addition to your monthly premium of $37.30, you may also need to pay:

  • Late enrollment penalty (LEP)
  • Part D income-related monthly adjustment amount

Paying your plan premium

You can pay your monthly plan premium (including any late enrollment penalty that you currently have or may owe) by mail, "Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT)" each month. You can also choose to pay your premium by having it automatically taken out of your Social Security or Railroad Retirement Board (RRB) benefit each month.

If you have to pay a Part D-Income Related Monthly Adjustment Amount (Part D-IRMAA), you must pay this extra amount in addition to your plan premium. The amount is usually taken out of your Social Security benefit, or you may get a bill from Medicare (or the RRB).
DO NOT pay McLaren the Part D-IRMAA.

I get monthly benefits from:
The Social Security/RRB deduction may take two or more months to begin after Social Security or RRB approves the deduction. In most cases, if Social Security or RRB accepts the enrollee’s request for automatic deduction, the first deduction from the enrollee’s Social Security or RRB benefit check will include all late enrollment penalties due from the enrollee’s enrollment effective date up to the point withholding begins. If Social Security or RRB does not approve the enrollee’s request for automatic deduction, we will send the enrollee a paper bill for their monthly late enrollment penalties.
